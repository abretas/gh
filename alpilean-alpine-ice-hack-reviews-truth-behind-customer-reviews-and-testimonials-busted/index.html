Most Common Weight Loss Mistakes To Avoid 
Losing weight is challenging, and most people lose this inspiration to lose weight because of some common mistakes they make. These mistakes are easily avoidable only if they are known in the first place. Read the following to get an idea of these common weight loss mistakes and how they may impact your weight loss progress. 
 
Relying on the weight scale 
Watching the weight scale is not always a reliable way of checking your weight-related progress. Many times weight scale does not indicate good progress, and it feels like you are doing something wrong. It is only one way to see how your body is responding to a certain diet or exercise regime, and in reality, the reading on the weight scale is checked by different factors, for example, fluid retention and the food inside your body. Interestingly, weight fluctuation between two and four pounds is common, especially in women where hormones affect weight gain and loss patterns. If you do not see much of a difference on your weight scale, it may indicate that the body is carrying water. People who work out in a routine sometimes gain muscles, which make very less change on the weight scale, giving an assumption that the body is probably not undergoing any progress. Check for all the risk factors during weight loss, including undigested food, lean mass gain, fluid retention, and others. 
 
Not eating enough calories 
Starving or eating lesser calories does not always mean the body is on a weight loss track. If you are eating too many or too few calories, the weight loss patterns change drastically. Health experts recommend following a calorie deficit plan, which does not mean starvation. It simply means to lose more calories than you are eating in the day. There is plenty of research evidence showing a certain amount of calories induces a natural fat burner, but recent researches reveal there is no ‘standard' calorie count, and it varies from person to person. 
 
In general, those following an active lifestyle with an addition of a daily activity burnt more calories. Many times healthy foods are also rich in calories, and digesting them doubles the work for the body. For this reason, moderate eating and daily portions are mandatory. During this process, do not cut the calories to a very low level, as it may put the body into fatigue and weakness. Eating lesser than your requirements can make the body lose muscle mass and decreases metabolism, which causes obesity. 
 
Inactive lifestyle 
It is also a common practice to see people complaining about their weight despite living a sedentary lifestyle. There is proven evidence on how not exercising or being active can affect your weight and restrict weight loss despite eating less. Without daily activity, the body starts losing fat instead of weight and becomes weak. Exercising helps it gain lean mass, which makes it easier to maintain weight. However, there should not be an overexercising routine. Moderation is the key, and it is better to follow a sustainable weight loss plan and not a quick remedy for overnight weight loss. 
 
Avoiding weight training 
Weight training and cardio are the two most popular exercise types, but there is a lot of misconception regarding which type of exercise is best for you. The scientific evidence suggests that weight training or resistance training can promote weight loss, and lifting weights improves the muscle gain capacity of the body. Contrary to the popular assumption, lifting weights does not make the body lethargic or cause weakness. For the best results, try using a metabolic booster, i.e., Alpilean pills with weight training, so that the body maintains the weight loss results for long. 
 
Misreading the labels 
People get confused by labels, i.e., ‘low-calorie,' ‘diet-friendly,' or ‘low-fat' foods. There is very little chance that these products are actually helpful in any way, and there is a high chance of an exactly opposite effect. These types of food contain high amounts of sugar, and salt, which is never a diet-friendly option. There is a limit to daily sugar consumption and when you choose these diet-friendly products, you ignore the amount of sugar in them. As a result, the daily caloric intake is changed, and the body starts gaining weight instead of losing it. 
 
Miscalculating the calories burnt 
Exercise seems a hard thing, and people often miscalculate the number of calories they burn during exercise. It is a well-known fact that exercise increases heart rate and metabolism, making the body burn more calories in less time. It is also possible that this change is much slower than your expectation. There are many studies showing that people often overestimate the exertion and calories that it burns. This miscalculation leads them to eat more than their dietary requirements; as a result, the body starts gaining weight, despite working out. The exercise or gym is not the real culprit here, but a misjudgment of the calories consumed and burnt. For best results, consult a fitness expert to devise a customized plan for your body, and eat in moderation despite the workout. 
 
Skipping protein-based foods
The idea of protein intake is linked with muscle gain, and it is the misconception that protein makes you bulky. The body needs a sufficient amount of protein to lose weight, too, because of its role in metabolism. The protein affects weight loss by acting upon appetite, satiety, metabolic rate, muscle mass, and digestion. Some of the protein-rich foods are quinoa, beans, flaxseeds, legumes, etc. Try to mix and match these dietary sources with other ingredients of your choice and create new recipes that are yummy and healthy.
