What Are Brown Fat Cells?

Brown adipose tissue (BAT) is a type of fat that helps to burn calories and regulate body temperature. Brown fat cells are unique because they contain more mitochondria than other fat cells. This allows them to burn more calories and produce more heat, making them important for regulating body temperature.
Studies have shown that a person with higher brown fat levels has a healthier body weight and is better able to burn off excess body fat. In fact, when researchers compared the brown fat levels of obese and lean individuals, they found that the obese individuals had significantly lower levels of brown fat. How Is Brown Fat Different From White Fat?

Brown fat is different from white fat in a few key ways. For starters, brown fat has a much higher density of mitochondria than white fat. This gives it the ability to burn more calories and stubborn fat layers and creates heat, which is why it’s often referred to as “good” fat.
In addition, brown fat cells are smaller than white fat cells. This also makes them more efficient at burning calories and burning body fat.
Another key difference is that cold temperatures more easily stimulate brown fat than white fat. This means that you can use brown fat to burn calories and help you lose weight by exposure to cold temperatures – something that white fat cannot do.
Lastly, while brown and white adipose tissue (fat) stores energy in triglycerides, brown adipose tissue also contains a protein called uncoupling protein 1 (UCP1).
This protein allows brown adipose tissue to burn more calories without producing ATP (energy). So, not only does brown adipose tissue help you lose weight by burning calories, but it also doesn’t produce any harmful byproducts like ATP.
