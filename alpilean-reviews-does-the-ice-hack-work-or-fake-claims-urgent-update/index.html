A study published in Molecular Nutrition & Food Research found that turmeric supplements may be beneficial for reducing waist circumference in adults who are overweight or obese. Researchers supplemented participants' diets with either a placebo or a supplement containing 500 milligrams of curcuminoids—the active components of turmeric—daily for 12 weeks and found that the group consuming antibodies lost an average of 1.2 centimeters of waist circumference compared to the placebo group, which lost an average of 0.3 centimeters during the same time frame.
A systematic review conducted by Healthline looked at five studies conducted over four years and concluded that taking curcumin supplements might help reduce body weight when combined with exercise.
In 4 out of 5 studies reviewed, taking 2-3 grams per day led to significant reductions in body weight compared to control groups who did not take supplements during the same period. However, further research is needed to determine long-term effectiveness.
