Peripheral neuropathy is a health condition suffered by many. It results from damaged nerves outside the brain and spinal cord. This condition causes extreme fatigue, numbness, and pain, especially in the feet and hands. It can also affect normal body processes such as digestion, urination, and blood circulation.
The peripheral nerves are crucial in transmitting signals from the brain through the nervous system to all body parts. The nerves also send sensory information from the body to the brain. Nerve damage can be caused by several factors, such as:
  • Injuries
  • Infections
  • Exposure to toxins
  • Diabetes
  • Metabolic issues
Damaged nerves cause peripheral neuropathy, often characterized by burning, stabbing, or tingling pain. Peripheral neuropathy has no cure. However, you can reduce the symptoms by treating the underlying condition. Most people use over-the-counter painkillers to help minimize pain. The common symptoms of neuropathy include:
  • Numbness, tingling, and prickling of the feet and hands
  • Sharp and throbbing pain
  • Sensitive to touch
  • Poor coordination can lead to a loss of balance
  • Weak muscles
  • In severe cases, it can cause paralysis
